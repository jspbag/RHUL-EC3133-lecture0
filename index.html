EC3133

EC3133 deepens your understanding of econometric theory and its applications, enabling you to appreciate and undertake applied economic work in the worlds of business and government policy.

EC3133 covers core econometric methods, including the estimation of causal effects using cross section data and panel data, and prediction with Big Data

EC3133 applications are developed using the econometric software R on RStudio

EC3133 learning objectives

  • Understand the theoretical properties of different econometric estimation and testing procedures under various modeling assumptions

  • Understand how to evaluate the appropriateness of econometric estimation methods under different data limitations and modeling assumptions

  • Be able to apply relevant econometric and statistical techniques to real data using econometric software (R)

Plan

  • Introducing R with RStudio

  • Review of cross section regression analysis

  • Regression with binary dependent variables

  • Panel data regressions

  • Instrumental variable regressions

  • Experiments and quasi-experiments

  • Prediction with many regressors and Big Data

R and RStudio

R is an open source programming language for statistical computing and graphics

R is widely used in academia and in the worlds of business and government

RStudio is a development environment for R
